ABOUT

I am a PhD candidate in philosophy at the University of Hamburg, supervised by Prof. Dr. Rolf W. Puster. My dissertation, Agency and Legitimate Governance: From Agency to the Limits to Government Intervention, develops a theory of political legitimacy grounded in action theory, asking where and why government intervention is legitimate.

My work sits at the intersection of political philosophy, Austrian school economics, and the history of political economy. I am particularly interested in the legitimacy of governance, comparative systems analysis (with a focus on interventionism), and the classical liberal tradition.

I am currently a Carl Menger Fellow at the Mercatus Center (2025–2026) and an EPICENTER Fellow (since 2025), and hold a bdoctoral scholarship from the Friedrich Naumann Foundation.

RESEARCH

Three of my chief research areas are:

  • Political Legitimacy & Action Theory: Starting from a theory of agency, I develop criteria for what counts as legitimate governance, focusing on the distinction between adversarial and non-adversarial interaction.
  • Descriptive Political Philosophy: Generalising on my scrutiny of the legitimacy of governance, I am researching the general contours of descriptive, value-free political philosophy.
  • Comparative Systems Analysis: Drawing on the Austrian tradition, I want to improve our understanding of different economic systems. Specifically, I want to apply analytic action theory and subjectivism to the dynamics of the mixed economy.

Current working papers include "Idealisations and Abstractions" (accepted with minor revisions, Journal for General Philosophy of Science), "Short-term and Long-term Stability" (under peer-review), and "A Concept in Need of Clarification: Authoritarian Liberalism."
